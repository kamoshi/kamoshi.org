---
title: 会う・遭う
---

1. 会う - the standard meeting

This is the kanji which is used 90% of the time. It implies a social encounter
or a planned meeting between people. It carries a neutral or positive
connotation.

* Meaning: To meet a person, to see a friend.
* Example: 週末に友達と会う約束があります。

2. 遭う - the unfortunate encounter

This kanji specifically denotes a chance encounter with something negative,
accidental, or disastrous. It is often used for accidents, rain, or being
victimized.

* Meaning: To encounter, to run into (trouble/disaster).
* Example: 旅行中に盗難に遭った。

***

[目]{め}に[遭]{あ}う is a fixed expression in Japanese. Even though [目]{め} usually
means "eye", in this context it refers to a "point in time" or an "experience."
It almost exclusively refers to negative experiences.
